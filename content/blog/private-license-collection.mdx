Dealership teams do not need another inbox full of blurry license photos. They need a repeatable way to ask for a document, help the customer create a usable PDF, and keep sensitive images out of unnecessary systems.

LicenseResizer is built around that boundary. The customer opens a branded link, captures the front of the license, optionally adds the back when the dealership asks for it, and creates the PDF locally in the browser.

## What changes for customers

The request feels more professional because the page belongs to the dealership experience. Customers see a simple flow instead of improvised instructions buried in a text thread.

They also stay in control of the final handoff. LicenseResizer can open the device share sheet, email draft, text draft, or download option, but the customer chooses what to send and where to send it.

## What changes for the store

The store gets a standard workflow without turning LicenseResizer into a document vault. Dealership settings, public handoff details, team roles, and lightweight activity events can support the process while license images and generated PDFs remain outside LicenseResizer storage.

## The operating principle

The simplest policy is the strongest one: do not collect the document if the product does not need to keep it. That keeps the customer experience focused and keeps the dealership handoff cleaner.
