Most license-copy problems are not dramatic. They are small workflow misses: a customer sends a cropped photo, a teammate forwards the wrong image, or someone has to explain how to print at actual size.

A better handoff starts before the customer takes the photo.

## Make the request recognizable

Use a dealership-branded link instead of a generic instruction. The page should tell customers what will happen, why the photo is needed, and how the final PDF will be shared.

## Keep the steps short

Customers should not have to understand image editing. A useful flow asks for the document, guides the capture, prepares the PDF, and then gets out of the way.

## Be precise about delivery

It is tempting to call every share action a delivery, but that creates the wrong expectation. A product can help prepare the file and open handoff options while still making clear that the customer controls the final recipient.

That wording matters. It keeps the team honest and keeps the customer aware of the last step.
